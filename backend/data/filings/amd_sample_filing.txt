ADVANCED MICRO DEVICES, INC. — Illustrative Form 10-K Excerpt (Sample
Fiscal Year)

Item 1. Business

Advanced Micro Devices designs high-performance and adaptive computing
products, including central processing units (CPUs), graphics processing
units (GPUs), and embedded processors, sold into client, gaming, data
center, and embedded end markets. In this illustrative fiscal year, Data
Center segment sample revenue was approximately $14.8 billion, up
roughly 94% over the prior illustrative period, reflecting increased
adoption of our Instinct accelerator product line by cloud and
enterprise customers. Client segment sample revenue was approximately
$7.1 billion, up modestly, as PC market conditions showed early signs of
stabilization. Gaming segment sample revenue, which includes
semi-custom console processors, declined approximately 12% on lower
console unit shipments in the illustrative period.

Item 1A. Risk Factors

Reliance on Third-Party Manufacturing. We do not own or operate our own
semiconductor fabrication facilities and depend on external foundries for
substantially all of our wafer supply. Constraints in leading-edge
process capacity, or unfavorable changes in our pricing arrangements
with foundry partners, could adversely affect our illustrative sample
gross margins and our ability to fulfill customer orders.

Intense Competition. We compete against larger, well-resourced
competitors across each of our product categories, including in
accelerated computing, where competitors have established software
ecosystems that create switching costs for customers. Our ability to
grow Data Center segment sample revenue depends in part on continued
investment in our own open software stack.

Customer and Market Concentration. A small number of hyperscale
customers represent a substantial and increasing share of our Data
Center segment's illustrative sample revenue. Additionally, our Gaming
segment's semi-custom sample revenue depends on a limited number of
console partners, and console generation transitions can create
significant period-over-period revenue volatility.

Inventory and Demand Forecasting. We must forecast demand for our
products several quarters in advance of shipment given foundry lead
times. Inaccurate forecasts, whether due to macroeconomic conditions or
rapidly shifting customer AI infrastructure spending plans, could result
in excess inventory or, alternatively, an inability to meet demand,
either of which could adversely affect our illustrative sample results.

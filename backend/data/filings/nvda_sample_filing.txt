Item 1. Business

NVIDIA Corporation designs and markets graphics processing units (GPUs),
data center accelerators, and associated software platforms used in
gaming, professional visualization, data center computing, and automotive
applications. In this illustrative fiscal year, the Data Center segment
generated approximately $47.2 billion in sample revenue, representing
growth of roughly 61% over the prior illustrative period, driven primarily
by demand for accelerated computing platforms used in training and
deploying large-scale AI models. Gaming segment sample revenue was
approximately $11.4 billion, essentially flat year-over-year, reflecting a
maturing consumer upgrade cycle offset by continued strength in

Item 1A. Risk Factors

Concentration of Suppliers. We depend on a small number of third-party
foundries and packaging partners to manufacture our products. Any
disruption at these facilities — whether from natural disaster, geopolitical
event, or capacity constraint — could materially impair our ability to
meet customer demand and could adversely affect our illustrative sample
revenue and margins.

Export Regulations. Our data center products are subject to evolving
export control regulations in the United States and other jurisdictions.
Changes to these regulations, including restrictions on sales of advanced
computing products to certain countries, could reduce our addressable
market and require us to develop region-specific product variants at

Customer Concentration. A limited number of large cloud service provider
customers account for a significant and growing portion of our Data
Center segment's sample revenue. The loss of, or a significant reduction

adverse effect on our illustrative results of operations.

Competitive Pressure. The market for accelerated computing is
increasingly competitive, with several well-capitalized competitors
investing heavily in custom silicon and alternative architectures. If we
are unable to maintain our technology leadership or pricing power, our
sample gross margins could decline in future periods.
